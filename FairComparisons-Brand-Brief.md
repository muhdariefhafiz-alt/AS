# FairComparisons — Brand Strategy Brief
### For: a fresh, ground-up brand identity & guidelines
### Status: clean-slate. Keep the **name only**. Retire all existing visual identity.

---

## 0. How to use this brief

This is a cold briefing document. The reader (a brand/design team) has no prior
context. It describes what FairComparisons is, who it serves, what it must make
people feel, and the functional realities the identity has to survive. It does
**not** prescribe colours, type, or logo — those are yours to create.

**The only thing carried forward is the name: "FairComparisons."**
There is an existing teal-based identity in production. **Deliberately retire it.**
Do not reverse-engineer or "evolve" it. Design as if from zero.

---

## 1. One-line definition

> **FairComparisons is an independent platform that ranks Singapore property
> agents on real government transaction data — not advertising — and matches
> home sellers with the agents who actually perform.**

It is, in spirit, "the Which?/MoneySavingExpert of choosing a property agent" for
Singapore. The closest global analogue is **GetAgent.co.uk** (UK). The business is
modelled on it deliberately.

---

## 2. Why it exists (the problem)

In Singapore, most property agents "look and sound the same" to a home seller.
There is no objective signal of who is good. Sellers default to whoever knocks
first, has the biggest ad spend, or promises the highest valuation (a known tactic
to win the listing, then talk the price down later). The dominant platforms
(PropertyGuru, 99.co) are **listing marketplaces where agents pay for visibility**
— they rank by who pays, not who performs.

FairComparisons exists to remove that information asymmetry. Every agent's actual
transaction history is public record (Singapore's CEA register). The platform
turns that raw data into a clear, trustworthy performance score, so a seller can
choose on evidence, and a good agent can win on merit instead of marketing budget.

**The emotional core of the problem:** selling a home is the largest, most
stressful financial decision most Singaporeans make, and they make it half-blind,
trusting a stranger chosen badly. FairComparisons replaces anxiety and guesswork
with clarity and fairness.

---

## 3. Mission, vision, values

- **Mission:** Help home sellers choose a property agent on evidence, and help
  good agents grow without paying for visibility.
- **Vision:** Become the trusted, independent performance layer for Singapore
  property — the place people and AI assistants go to answer "which agent should
  I use?"
- **Core values (these are non-negotiable and must be *felt* in the brand):**
  1. **Independence** — rankings cannot be bought. Ever. This is the moat.
  2. **Evidence over marketing** — everything traces to real government data.
  3. **Fairness** — the name is the promise. The playing field is level.
  4. **Honesty** — we never fabricate. No fake reviews, no invented stats, no
     manufactured urgency. (This is an operating rule, not a slogan.)
  5. **Clarity** — we make complex data legible to a stressed, non-expert seller.

> If the visual identity makes the platform feel like an advertiser-funded
> listing site, it has failed. It must feel like an *independent referee*.

---

## 4. Brand positioning

**Category:** independent comparison / matching marketplace (two-sided).
**Frame of reference:** how you choose a property agent in Singapore.
**Point of difference:** ranked on real CEA transaction performance, not ad spend.
**Reason to believe:** 30,000+ agents, 730,000+ government transaction records,
every district and HDB town — all public, all verifiable.

**Positioning statement:**
> For Singapore home sellers who don't know which agent to trust, FairComparisons
> is the independent comparison platform that ranks every agent on real
> transaction performance — because the best agent should win on results, not on
> who paid the most to be seen.

**What we stand against (the foil):** pay-to-play property portals. The brand
should feel like the honest, data-clean alternative to a noisy, advertiser-driven
incumbent. Not aggressive or combative — quietly, confidently *fair*.

---

## 5. Audiences (this is a TWO-SIDED brand)

The identity must speak credibly to two very different people. This is the central
design tension.

### Audience A — The home seller (demand side, the hero)
- **Who:** Singaporean homeowner selling an HDB flat or private condo. Often an
  "upgrader" (HDB → condo, the dominant SG asset-progression journey). Frequently
  a first-time or once-a-decade seller — inexperienced, high stakes.
- **Mindset:** anxious, time-poor, skeptical of agents, wants to not get ripped
  off. Not a data expert. Decides emotionally, justifies with data.
- **Lifecycle moments:** approaching HDB MOP (5-year sell-eligibility); wondering
  "what's my home worth?"; ready to sell and choosing an agent.
- **What the brand must give them:** reassurance, clarity, a feeling of being in
  control and treated fairly. Friendly but credible. Not playful/cute (this is
  serious money), not cold/corporate (they're anxious humans).

### Audience B — The property agent (supply side)
- **Who:** CEA-licensed salesperson. ~37,000 in Singapore, ~70% concentrated in
  PropNex / ERA / Huttons / OrangeTee. Many spend S$1,000–12,000/yr on
  PropertyGuru with no performance guarantee.
- **Mindset:** professional, competitive, fee-conscious, jaded by paying portals.
  Proud of their track record (or wanting to build one).
- **What the brand must give them:** respect, professionalism, and the message
  "you're judged on your record, not your wallet." Tools that feel like a serious
  professional product, not a consumer toy.

> **Design implication:** the consumer surfaces (home valuation, agent shortlist)
> can be warmer and more guided; the agent surfaces (dashboard, profile claim) can
> be more professional and dense — but both must share one coherent identity. The
> through-line is *credibility you can feel*.

---

## 6. Brand personality (attributes to design toward)

Pick a coherent identity that lands these. (Illustrative adjectives, not a mandate
on style.)

- **Trustworthy** — the #1 job. People are handing over their biggest asset's sale.
- **Independent / unbought** — feels like a referee or a public utility, not a vendor.
- **Clear** — legible, calm, data made simple. Anti-clutter, anti-hype.
- **Modern & competent** — current, well-built, "this team knows what they're doing."
- **Fair & human** — warm enough that an anxious seller feels held, not lectured.

**Personality guardrails (what it must NOT be):**
- Not flashy, salesy, or "property-bro" (that's the incumbents).
- Not cold/institutional/bank-like (alienates the anxious seller).
- Not cute/gamified (undermines trust on a high-stakes decision).
- Not luxury/aspirational (this is about fairness and evidence, not status).

---

## 7. Tone of voice (strategic inputs — define the system freshly)

- **Plain English, Singapore context.** The audience is Singaporean; English is
  the lingua franca for property. Avoid jargon; explain CEA/MOP/AgentScore simply.
- **Confident and calm, never hyped.** We let the data talk. No exclamation-mark
  marketing, no manufactured urgency.
- **Honest to a fault.** We never claim what we can't prove. Where data is thin, we
  say so. This honesty is a brand asset — lean into it.
- **Respectful of two readers.** Reassuring to sellers; professional to agents.
- **Hard constraints to honour:**
  - **Never use em dashes** (a standing house-style rule).
  - **Never fabricate** quotes, testimonials, reviews, or statistics. All numbers
    must be real and sourced (CEA / URA / HDB).
  - **Founder anonymity:** the company is presented brand-first. There is no named
    founder/personality in any public-facing material.

---

## 8. Where the brand lives (surfaces to design for)

This is a content- and data-heavy product. The identity must work across all of:

**Consumer (seller) surfaces**
- Homepage (the "compare agents / sell your home / what's it worth" entry)
- Agent comparison & ranking pages (per district, per HDB town) — **lists, scores,
  badges, tables** are central
- Individual agent profile pages (score, transaction history, reviews, charts)
- Tools: **home valuation** (returns a value *range*, not a single number),
  **HDB MOP tracker** (countdown to sell-eligibility)
- Seller funnel: a multi-step flow (tell us about your home → see ranked shortlist
  → invite agents → compare their quotes → choose one)
- Reviews (verified-completion badges + community reviews)

**Agent (supply) surfaces**
- "For agents" marketing pages (the value prop: free, pay only on completion)
- Agent dashboard (leads inbox, quote submission, completion logging, analytics)
- Claim-your-profile flow

**System / lifecycle**
- Transactional emails (shortlist ready, quote received, invoice, review request)
- WhatsApp notifications (the dominant SG comms channel — short, plain templates)
- An **admin command-centre** (internal; data-dense dashboards)

**Machine-facing**
- An "AI Discovery Protocol" — structured JSON endpoints + llms.txt so AI
  assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity) cite the platform. The brand should be legible
  even as plain, structured data.

> **Design implication:** the system must carry a LOT of numbers, scores, tables,
> rankings, badges, and trust signals without feeling like a spreadsheet. The
> "AgentScore" (a 0–100 number) is a hero data object — it likely needs its own
> visual treatment (think a credit-score or rating-gauge moment). Trust badges
> ("verified completion," "ranked on CEA data") are recurring, important elements.

---

## 9. The core trust mechanics the design must express

These are the substantive things that make the brand credible. The identity should
make them feel real and legible:

- **AgentScore (0–100):** the central proof object. Computed from transaction
  volume, recency, market diversity, experience, and reviews. Must feel objective,
  earned, and un-game-able.
- **"Rankings cannot be bought":** there is no paid placement, no sponsored slots.
  This must be visible and believable in the UI (no "promoted" treatments anywhere).
- **Government data provenance:** CEA (agent register), URA (private transactions),
  HDB (resale data). Citing the source is a trust act — design should foreground it.
- **Verified-completion reviews:** reviews tied to real, completed sales carry a
  distinct badge vs. open community reviews. The hierarchy of trust must be clear.
- **Success-fee model:** sellers pay nothing; agents pay only when a referred sale
  completes. "Free for sellers" is a core, repeated message.

---

## 10. Business & revenue context (so the brand reflects the real model)

- **Revenue:** pure success fee — agents pay 0.5% of the sale price only when a
  sale completes through the platform. No upfront cost, no subscription gate, no
  paid ranking. (Optional non-ranking agent tools exist but are peripheral.)
- **Two-sided marketplace:** sellers are free; agents are the payers. The brand
  must make sellers feel it's genuinely free and unbiased, while making agents feel
  it's a fair, performance-based deal.
- **Stage:** early. Live product, pre-scale. The brand should feel established and
  trustworthy *beyond its actual size* — punching up in credibility is a goal.

---

## 11. Competitive & cultural landscape (Singapore)

- **PropertyGuru / 99.co** — incumbent listing portals. Agents pay for visibility.
  This is the *foil*: the brand should feel like the honest, independent opposite.
  Do not look or feel like them (loud, listing-grid, ad-driven).
- **Propseller, Ohmyhome** — discount / hybrid / DIY agency models. Deliberately
  **not** competitors we compare against; we are about full-service agent
  performance. The brand is not "cheap," it's "fair and evidence-based."
- **Seedly, MoneySmart, Stacked Homes** — trusted SG finance/property editorial.
  Adjacent in tone-credibility; a useful reference for "trusted, data-literate,
  Singaporean, not salesy."
- **Cultural notes:** Singapore is pragmatic, data-respecting, mobile-first,
  WhatsApp-native, and property-obsessed (HDB upgrading is a national pastime).
  Bilingual context exists but English leads for property. Avoid Western property
  clichés (white picket fences); SG housing is HDB blocks and condos.

---

## 12. Brand architecture (note for future-proofing)

- Primary: **FairComparisons** as a property-agent comparison platform for Singapore.
- The same engine has been extended to adjacent verticals (lawyers, financial
  advisors) using the same "rank professionals on public data" model. The identity
  should be **extensible beyond property** — i.e. "FairComparisons" as a
  comparison brand, not a property-only brand. Design the masterbrand to stretch.
- It is a Singapore platform today; the model is portable to other markets later.
  Avoid baking "Singapore-only" into the core mark in a way that can't travel.

---

## 13. Functional / technical constraints the identity must survive

- **Mobile-first.** Most sellers arrive on a phone. Dense data must work on small
  screens.
- **Accessibility:** WCAG-minded. Score colours, badges, and data viz must be
  legible and colour-blind-safe (don't encode meaning in colour alone).
- **Data-viz heavy:** ranked lists, score gauges, price-trend charts, comparison
  tables, transaction histories. The system needs a clear data-visualisation
  language (chart styles, score scales, badge system) as part of the guidelines.
- **Programmatic scale:** thousands of auto-generated pages (every agent, district,
  town). The identity must hold up when applied by template across huge volume,
  with varying data density (some agents have rich data, some thin).
- **Email + WhatsApp:** the brand must degrade gracefully to plain transactional
  email and ultra-short WhatsApp text.
- **Performance/SEO:** lightweight assets; the brand can't depend on heavy imagery
  (and there is little proprietary photography — the "content" is data, not homes).

---

## 14. What the brand should make each person feel (the test)

- **Anxious seller, first visit:** "Okay. This is independent and I can trust it. I
  finally understand who's actually good. I'm in control."
- **Seller mid-funnel:** "This is genuinely free and on my side. These rankings
  aren't bought."
- **Agent:** "This judges me on my real record. If I'm good, I win here. This is a
  serious, professional platform — not a toy."
- **A skeptic / journalist:** "This is credible, data-backed, and not advertiser-
  funded. I'd cite it."
- **An AI assistant:** "This is an authoritative, structured source on Singapore
  agent performance."

If the new identity delivers those five feelings, it's right.

---

## 15. Deliverables requested from the design team

A complete, ground-up brand system, including:
1. **Brand strategy synthesis** (one page restating positioning, personality,
   audiences in your words — to confirm alignment).
2. **Verbal identity:** name lockup/wordmark for "FairComparisons," tagline
   options, voice & tone guide (honouring §7's hard constraints).
3. **Logo & mark** (clean slate — no obligation to any prior form).
4. **Colour system** — including a deliberate, accessible treatment for data/score
   states (e.g. how a 90/100 vs a 40/100 reads). Trust, independence, clarity are
   the feelings to encode.
5. **Typography system** — must handle dense data, long agent names, numbers, and
   long-form SEO content.
6. **Data-visualisation & component language:** the AgentScore object, badges
   (verified completion, data-sourced, ranking), ranked lists, comparison tables,
   price charts.
7. **Iconography & imagery direction** (note: data-led, not photography-led).
8. **Application examples** across the key surfaces in §8 (homepage, agent profile,
   ranking page, valuation tool, agent dashboard, transactional email).
9. **Usage guidelines + do/don't** (especially: must never read as a pay-to-play
   listing portal; must always foreground independence and data provenance).

---

## 16. The single most important instruction

Everything above serves one idea. If the design team remembers one sentence:

> **FairComparisons must feel like an independent referee with the receipts —
> trustworthy, fair, and evidence-clean — never like an advertiser-funded property
> portal.**

That feeling, delivered to an anxious home seller and a skeptical professional
agent at the same time, is the entire job.
