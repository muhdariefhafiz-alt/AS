# Distribution addendum: "Which Property Agency Is Best?" study (Jul 2026)

Companion to `study-distribution-kit.md` (same targets, same guardrails). This
adds the second pitchable asset published 2 Jul 2026:

**The asset:** https://fair-comparisons.com/insights/best-property-agency-singapore

Why it is separately pitchable: the agent-statistics study is a market-wide
curiosity ("most agents barely sell"); this one names the household brands
consumers actually choose between, so it is a consumer-service story, not just
a data curiosity. Different editors, different angle, same dataset.

**The hook stats (all real, rendered live on the page from the daily-refreshed
cache; re-check the page before sending because figures update):**
- Only 307 of roughly 930 licensed agencies recorded even one home sale in the
  last 12 months. Only about ten recorded a hundred or more.
- The volume leader recorded 10,210 home sales, but only 15.5% of its 14,539
  registered agents recorded a single home sale, and 68.6% of its recorded
  activity is rentals.
- The four biggest brands average about 3.9 home sales per selling agent.
- The productivity leaders are small: the top agency by sales-per-selling-agent
  recorded 47.7 home sales per selling agent, roughly twelve times the
  big-brand average.
- Conclusion the data supports: the productive unit in Singapore property is
  the individual agent, not the agency brand.

**Guardrails (same as the main kit, plus one):**
- Every number must trace to the live study page. No invented stats.
- Do not name the founder anywhere. Attribute to FairComparisons. Sign
  first-name-only if a name is unavoidable.
- Strictly factual and neutral about named agencies: all figures are counts
  from the public CEA record, stated without characterization of any firm.
  Say "recorded no home sale in the window in the CEA record", never anything
  that reads as an accusation. The methodology caveats (current-agency
  attribution, CEA publication lag, sides not deals) are ON the page; link it
  so editors see them.
- Value-first, disclose affiliation in communities.

Hi [name],

We analysed the public CEA transaction record and built the first league table
of Singapore's property agencies on actual recorded home sales rather than
marketing claims. Three findings your readers may find useful:

1. Only 307 of roughly 930 licensed agencies recorded even one home sale in
   the last 12 months, and only about ten recorded a hundred or more.
2. The biggest brand tops the volume chart by a wide margin, but only 15.5% of
   its 14,539 registered agents recorded a single home sale in the window, and
   over two-thirds of its recorded activity is rentals. The pattern holds
   across the four biggest brands.
3. Sales per selling agent turns the table upside down: small, focused
   agencies close around ten times more homes per agent than the big-brand
   average.

Full tables, method and caveats (including why deals are attributed to an
agent's current agency and what the CEA publication lag does to recent
months): https://fair-comparisons.com/insights/best-property-agency-singapore

Happy to share the underlying aggregates or cut the data by property type or
town if useful. Everything traces to the public CEA record.
